## How to Use This Swipe File

These templates are starting points, not scripts to copy verbatim. For every email you send:

1. **Research first.** Spend 5-10 minutes on the prospect's website, LinkedIn, and recent news before writing.
2. **Personalize the bracketed sections.** Every `[PLACEHOLDER]` must be replaced with specific, genuine details. Generic emails get deleted. Specific emails get replies.
3. **Send from a warm domain.** If you are using a new email address, warm it up for 2-3 weeks before outreach (send and receive normal emails, subscribe to newsletters, reply to threads).
4. **Follow up.** 80% of replies come from follow-ups. Send 2-3 follow-ups spaced 3-5 business days apart.
5. **Track everything.** Log every email in your pipeline tracker (see the Pipeline Tracker bonus).

**Formatting rules:**
- Keep emails under 150 words (shorter = higher reply rates)
- No images, no HTML formatting, no signatures with logos
- Write like a human, not a marketer
- One clear call to action per email
- Never lie or fabricate compliments

## Template 1: The "Noticed Something" Email

**When to use it:** You found something specific on their website, social media, or in their business that you can genuinely comment on. This works best when your observation connects directly to the service you provide.

**Subject line:**
- `[SPECIFIC THING] on [THEIR COMPANY NAME]'s site`
- Alt 1: `Quick thought on your [SPECIFIC AREA]`
- Alt 2: `Noticed this about [THEIR COMPANY NAME]`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> I was looking at [THEIR COMPANY NAME]'s [WEBSITE / SOCIAL MEDIA / SPECIFIC PAGE] and noticed [SPECIFIC OBSERVATION — e.g., "your checkout flow has 5 steps before payment," "your blog hasn't been updated since March," "your landing page doesn't mention the feature you just launched on Product Hunt"].
>
> I mention it because I work with [TYPE OF BUSINESS — e.g., "SaaS companies in the project management space"] on [WHAT YOU DO — e.g., "conversion rate optimization"], and that's usually one of the first things I'd look at.
>
> Not trying to pitch you — just thought it was worth flagging. If you'd ever want a second set of eyes on [SPECIFIC AREA], I'm happy to share a few ideas. No strings attached.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** Specificity proves you did your homework. Leading with a genuine observation (not a compliment) positions you as someone who notices details others miss, which is exactly what clients want in a freelancer.

## Template 2: The "Quick Question" Email

**When to use it:** When you want to start a conversation with minimal friction. This works for high-volume outreach because it is short and low-commitment. Best for prospects you have moderate knowledge about.

**Subject line:**
- `Quick question`
- Alt 1: `Question about [THEIR INITIATIVE / PRODUCT / STRATEGY]`
- Alt 2: `Curious about something`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Are you currently [DOING THE THING YOUR SERVICE RELATES TO — e.g., "investing in content marketing," "redesigning your mobile experience," "running paid acquisition campaigns"]?
>
> I ask because I help [TYPE OF CLIENT] with [YOUR SPECIFIC SERVICE], and I had a couple of ideas that might be relevant for [THEIR COMPANY NAME].
>
> If you're the right person to talk to, I'd love 15 minutes on your calendar this week. If not, who would you point me to?
>
> Thanks,
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** Questions trigger a psychological response — people feel compelled to answer. The "if not, who should I talk to?" line gives them an easy out that still moves you forward by getting a referral to the right person.

## Template 3: The "Mutual Connection" Email

**When to use it:** You share a community, Slack group, LinkedIn connection, conference, mastermind, or any other context with the prospect. The connection does not need to be strong — even being in the same Slack workspace or having attended the same event counts.

**Subject line:**
- `[MUTUAL CONNECTION NAME / COMMUNITY NAME] → quick intro`
- Alt 1: `Fellow [COMMUNITY / GROUP NAME] member`
- Alt 2: `[MUTUAL CONNECTION NAME] suggested I reach out`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> We're both [IN THE SAME COMMUNITY / CONNECTED THROUGH MUTUAL CONNECTION / ATTENDED THE SAME EVENT — e.g., "in the SaaS Growth Hackers Slack group," "connected through Sarah Chen at Acme Corp," "at MicroConf last October"].
>
> I've been following [THEIR COMPANY NAME]'s [SOMETHING SPECIFIC — e.g., "growth over the past year," "recent product launch," "content strategy"], and I think there might be an opportunity for us to work together.
>
> I specialize in [YOUR SERVICE] for [THEIR TYPE OF BUSINESS], and I recently [BRIEF CREDIBILITY MARKER — e.g., "helped a similar company increase their trial-to-paid conversion by 34%," "redesigned the onboarding flow for a Series A fintech app"].
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week to see if there's a fit?
>
> [YOUR NAME]
>
> P.S. [OPTIONAL: PERSONAL TOUCH — e.g., "Loved your talk at MicroConf about pricing experiments, by the way. We actually implemented a version of your approach with a client."]

**Why it works:** Shared context transforms you from a stranger into a familiar name. The P.S. line adds a human touch that makes the email feel like a genuine reach-out rather than a mass campaign.

## Template 4: The "Case Study" Email

**When to use it:** You have a strong result from a past project with a client who is similar to the prospect (same industry, same company size, same type of problem). This is your highest-converting template when the case study is relevant.

**Subject line:**
- `How [SIMILAR COMPANY / INDUSTRY] got [SPECIFIC RESULT]`
- Alt 1: `[SPECIFIC RESULT] for [SIMILAR COMPANY TYPE]`
- Alt 2: `Thought this result would interest you`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> I recently worked with [PAST CLIENT NAME or "a [DESCRIPTION OF SIMILAR COMPANY]" if confidential] on [THE PROJECT — e.g., "redesigning their email onboarding sequence," "rebuilding their pricing page," "launching a content engine"].
>
> The result: [SPECIFIC, QUANTIFIED RESULT — e.g., "trial-to-paid conversion went from 8% to 14% in 6 weeks," "organic traffic grew 3x in 4 months," "they cut their customer acquisition cost by 40%"].
>
> I noticed [THEIR COMPANY NAME] is [WHAT MAKES THEM SIMILAR — e.g., "in a similar space," "at a similar growth stage," "facing some of the same challenges based on what I've seen on your site"].
>
> I'm not sure if the same approach would apply to your situation, but I'd be happy to walk you through what we did and whether it could translate. Would a 15-minute call be worth your time?
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** Concrete results for a similar company make the prospect think, "If it worked for them, it could work for me." The humility of "I'm not sure if the same approach would apply" removes sales pressure and increases trust.

## Template 5: The "Helpful Resource" Email

**When to use it:** When you want to play the long game and build goodwill before asking for anything. Send this to high-value prospects you want to warm up. The resource must be genuinely useful — not a thinly veiled sales pitch disguised as content.

**Subject line:**
- `[RESOURCE TOPIC] — thought this would be useful`
- Alt 1: `Resource for [THEIR SPECIFIC CHALLENGE]`
- Alt 2: `This might help with [SPECIFIC THING]`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> I've been working in [YOUR SPACE — e.g., "conversion optimization for SaaS companies"] for [X YEARS], and I recently put together [DESCRIBE THE RESOURCE — e.g., "a checklist of the 15 highest-impact changes I've seen on pricing pages," "a short guide on the email sequences that actually reduce churn," "a teardown of 5 onboarding flows that convert above 20%"].
>
> Given what [THEIR COMPANY NAME] is doing with [SPECIFIC DETAIL — e.g., "your new self-serve pricing tier," "your expansion into the enterprise market"], I thought you might find it useful.
>
> Here's the link: [LINK TO RESOURCE — Google Doc, blog post, PDF, Loom video]
>
> No catch, no pitch. Just something I think is relevant to what you're building.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Follow-up (send 4-5 days later):**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Wanted to make sure [RESOURCE NAME] landed in your inbox. Did you get a chance to look at it?
>
> If any of those [TIPS / STRATEGIES / IDEAS] resonate and you'd want to talk through how they'd apply to [THEIR COMPANY NAME], I'm happy to jump on a quick call.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** Giving before asking activates the reciprocity principle. When you lead with genuine value, the prospect's guard drops and they associate you with expertise instead of spam. The follow-up introduces the call request only after you have already provided something useful.

## Template 6: The "Trigger Event" Email

**When to use it:** The prospect just experienced a notable event — a new funding round, a job posting, a product launch, a leadership change, a press mention, or an industry shift. Timing is everything with this template. Send it within 48 hours of the event.

**Subject line:**
- `Congrats on [THE EVENT] — quick thought`
- Alt 1: `Saw the news about [THEIR COMPANY NAME]`
- Alt 2: `[THE EVENT] + an idea`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Saw that [THEIR COMPANY NAME] just [THE TRIGGER EVENT — e.g., "closed a $5M Series A," "posted a job for a Head of Marketing," "launched on Product Hunt," "was featured in TechCrunch"]. Congratulations — that's a big milestone.
>
> In my experience working with [TYPE OF COMPANY] at this stage, [INSIGHT ABOUT WHAT TYPICALLY HAPPENS NEXT — e.g., "the next bottleneck is usually converting the traffic spike into retained users," "the biggest lever is usually getting the positioning right before scaling paid acquisition," "companies usually need to level up their [YOUR AREA] to support the growth the new funding enables"].
>
> I specialize in [YOUR SERVICE] and have helped [1-2 SIMILAR COMPANIES OR A BRIEF RESULT] navigate this exact phase.
>
> Would it make sense to chat for 15 minutes about what's on your roadmap? Happy to share what I've seen work.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** Trigger events signal that a company is actively investing, growing, or changing — which means budgets are in motion and new needs are emerging. Demonstrating that you understand what comes next positions you as a strategic partner, not just a vendor.

## Template 7: The "Before and After" Email

**When to use it:** When you want to paint a vivid picture of the transformation your service provides. This works especially well for services where the outcome is visible or measurable — design, conversion optimization, copywriting, automation, etc.

**Subject line:**
- `What [THEIR AREA] could look like in [TIMEFRAME]`
- Alt 1: `The gap between [CURRENT STATE] and [DESIRED STATE]`
- Alt 2: `A different version of [SPECIFIC AREA OF THEIR BUSINESS]`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Right now, [DESCRIBE THEIR CURRENT SITUATION BASED ON YOUR RESEARCH — e.g., "your landing page is asking visitors to do too much at once — there are 4 CTAs above the fold and the value prop is buried," "your blog is publishing consistently, but the posts aren't targeting keywords with commercial intent," "your onboarding emails are all product-focused with no personalization based on use case"].
>
> Here's what "after" could look like:
>
> [DESCRIBE THE TRANSFORMED STATE — e.g., "A single, focused landing page that guides visitors from headline → social proof → CTA, with conversion rates above 5%," "A content strategy that brings in 500+ qualified visitors per month who are actively looking for a solution like yours," "An onboarding sequence that segments users by goal and delivers relevant value in the first 48 hours, cutting churn by 20-30%"].
>
> I've made this exact shift for [SIMILAR CLIENT OR TYPE OF COMPANY], and the impact was [SPECIFIC RESULT].
>
> If this sounds interesting, I'd love 15 minutes to show you how I'd approach it for [THEIR COMPANY NAME].
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** The contrast between "before" and "after" creates a gap that the prospect wants to close. By describing their current state accurately (which requires research), you demonstrate that you already understand their world — making them trust that you can deliver the transformation.

## Template 8: The "Straight Shooter" Email

**When to use it:** For busy executives, founders, and decision-makers who do not have time for pleasantries. This template respects their time by being direct about who you are, what you do, and what you want. Use this when you are confident in your value proposition.

**Subject line:**
- `[YOUR SERVICE] for [THEIR COMPANY NAME]`
- Alt 1: `15 minutes?`
- Alt 2: `[YOUR NAME] — [YOUR SERVICE]`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> I'll keep this short.
>
> I'm [YOUR NAME], a [YOUR TITLE / SPECIALTY — e.g., "freelance conversion copywriter," "UX designer specializing in SaaS onboarding," "growth marketing consultant"]. I help [TYPE OF CLIENTS] [ACHIEVE WHAT — e.g., "turn more trial users into paying customers," "build content engines that drive qualified leads," "fix the leaks in their sales funnels"].
>
> [ONE SENTENCE OF PROOF — e.g., "Last quarter, I helped a B2B SaaS company increase demo requests by 47% with a landing page rewrite," "I recently reduced onboarding drop-off by 35% for a fintech startup"].
>
> I think I could help [THEIR COMPANY NAME]. Can we do 15 minutes this week?
>
> [YOUR NAME]
> [YOUR WEBSITE OR PORTFOLIO LINK]

**Why it works:** In a sea of long, meandering cold emails, brevity stands out. Busy people appreciate directness. This template signals confidence — you are not apologizing for reaching out or burying your ask under layers of preamble.

## Template 9: The "LinkedIn-to-Email Bridge" Email

**When to use it:** After you have had a genuine interaction on LinkedIn — they liked your post, you commented on theirs, you exchanged messages, or you connected and had a brief chat. This is NOT for cold LinkedIn connections where no interaction occurred.

**Subject line:**
- `Following up from LinkedIn`
- Alt 1: `Moving our conversation off LinkedIn`
- Alt 2: `[FIRST NAME] — from our LinkedIn exchange`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> We [DESCRIBE THE LINKEDIN INTERACTION — e.g., "exchanged a few comments on your post about remote team management last week," "connected after your talk at the virtual SaaS summit," "had a quick chat in the DMs about your upcoming product launch"].
>
> I wanted to follow up here because [REASON — e.g., "I had a more detailed thought I didn't want to squeeze into a LinkedIn message," "I think there might be a real opportunity to work together," "I wanted to share something more specific about what I do"].
>
> [TRANSITION TO YOUR VALUE — e.g., "You mentioned struggling with [SPECIFIC CHALLENGE]. That's exactly what I help [TYPE OF COMPANY] solve. I recently worked with [SIMILAR CLIENT] on the same problem and [RESULT]."]
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute call to explore this? I have availability [DAY] and [DAY] this week.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** Moving from a social platform to email signals seriousness. The prospect already recognizes your name, which dramatically increases open rates. Referencing the specific interaction proves you are not sending a mass template.

## Template 10: The "Past Client Revival" Email

**When to use it:** For re-engaging a client you have not worked with in 3+ months. Past clients are your warmest leads — they already know your work quality, your communication style, and your reliability. This is the easiest outreach you will ever do.

**Subject line:**
- `Been a while, [FIRST NAME]`
- Alt 1: `Checking in on [THEIR COMPANY NAME]`
- Alt 2: `Something that made me think of you`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> It's been a while since we wrapped up [THE LAST PROJECT — e.g., "the website redesign," "the email sequence project," "the brand identity work"]. I hope things have been going well at [THEIR COMPANY NAME].
>
> I've been thinking about [SOMETHING RELEVANT — e.g., "the landing pages we built and some new conversion strategies I've been testing with other clients," "your content strategy now that you've had a few months of data," "how your onboarding flow has been performing since we launched it"].
>
> Since we worked together, I've [NEW SKILL, SERVICE, OR RESULT — e.g., "started offering A/B testing as part of my conversion packages," "helped two other companies in your space increase retention by 25%+," "developed a new framework for scaling content production"].
>
> If [THEIR COMPANY NAME] has any upcoming needs in [YOUR AREA], I'd love to catch up and see if there's a way to work together again. Any interest in a quick call?
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** Familiarity eliminates the trust barrier. By referencing the specific project you completed and introducing a new capability, you give them a reason to re-engage beyond just "checking in." You are not begging for work — you are offering evolved value.

## Template 11: The "Referral Introduction" Email

**When to use it:** Someone told you to reach out to this person. Referral emails have 3-5x higher response rates than cold outreach. Always name the referrer (with their permission) and be specific about why they made the connection.

**Subject line:**
- `[REFERRER NAME] suggested we connect`
- Alt 1: `Introduction via [REFERRER NAME]`
- Alt 2: `[REFERRER NAME] said I should reach out`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> [REFERRER NAME] suggested I reach out to you. [CONTEXT — e.g., "We were chatting about the challenges SaaS companies face with user onboarding, and your name came up," "I've been doing some work for [REFERRER'S COMPANY] on their conversion funnel, and [REFERRER] mentioned you might be dealing with something similar," "We were at [EVENT] together and when I described what I do, they immediately thought of you"].
>
> A bit about me: I'm a [YOUR SPECIALTY] who works with [TYPE OF CLIENT] to [OUTCOME — e.g., "improve their conversion rates," "build scalable content strategies," "design products that retain users"]. [ONE PROOF POINT — e.g., "Most recently, I helped [REFERRER'S COMPANY or ANOTHER CLIENT] achieve [RESULT]."]
>
> [REFERRER NAME] thought there might be a fit, and I'd love to learn more about what [THEIR COMPANY NAME] is working on. Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week?
>
> [YOUR NAME]
>
> P.S. Happy to have [REFERRER NAME] make a formal intro if you'd prefer — just wanted to keep it simple.

**Why it works:** A referral transfers trust. When someone the prospect already respects vouches for you, the psychological barrier to responding drops dramatically. The P.S. offering a formal introduction gives the prospect an easy on-ramp if they want more social proof.

## Template 12: The "Breakup" Email

**When to use it:** This is your final follow-up after 2-3 previous emails that received no response. Send it 5-7 days after your last follow-up. It works because people hate losing options — even options they have been ignoring.

**Subject line:**
- `Should I close your file?`
- Alt 1: `One last thing`
- Alt 2: `Closing the loop`

**Email body:**

> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> I've reached out a couple of times about [BRIEF REFERENCE TO YOUR SERVICE / OFFER — e.g., "helping [THEIR COMPANY NAME] with conversion optimization," "the content strategy idea I shared"]. I haven't heard back, which is totally fine — I know you're busy.
>
> I don't want to be the person who keeps cluttering your inbox, so I'll assume the timing isn't right and stop reaching out.
>
> If [THE NEED — e.g., "improving your conversion rates," "scaling your content," "redesigning your onboarding"] ever moves up the priority list, you know where to find me.
>
> Wishing you and the [THEIR COMPANY NAME] team all the best.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

**Why it works:** The breakup email consistently outperforms every other email in a follow-up sequence. It triggers loss aversion — the prospect suddenly realizes they are about to lose access to you, which often prompts a response even when all previous emails were ignored. The key is genuine sincerity. Do not be passive-aggressive. Actually mean it when you wish them well.

## AI Prompt: Generate a Personalized Cold Email

Use this prompt with ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI assistant to generate a customized version of any template above:

```
I need to write a cold email to a prospective client. Here are the details:

**My info:**
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My service: [WHAT YOU DO]
- My specialty/niche: [YOUR NICHE]
- A relevant result I've achieved: [SPECIFIC RESULT WITH NUMBERS]

**Prospect info:**
- Their name: [PROSPECT NAME]
- Their company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Their role: [THEIR TITLE]
- Their industry: [INDUSTRY]
- What I noticed about their business: [SPECIFIC OBSERVATION FROM YOUR RESEARCH]
- Why I think I can help them: [THE CONNECTION BETWEEN THEIR NEED AND YOUR SERVICE]

**Template style:** [CHOOSE: Noticed Something / Quick Question / Mutual Connection / Case Study / Helpful Resource / Trigger Event / Before and After / Straight Shooter / LinkedIn Bridge / Past Client Revival / Referral Intro / Breakup]

Write a cold email under 150 words that is specific, human, and ends with a clear call to action. Do not use hype words, exclamation points, or marketing jargon. Write it in a conversational, professional tone.
```
