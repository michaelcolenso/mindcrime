## Why You Need a Pipeline Tracker

Most freelancers have no idea where their next client is coming from. They rely on luck, referrals, and the anxiety of an empty inbox. A pipeline tracker fixes this by giving you:

- **Visibility.** You can see exactly how many prospects are at each stage at any moment.
- **Predictability.** When you know your conversion rates, you can forecast revenue weeks in advance.
- **Accountability.** A weekly review forces you to take consistent action instead of binging and purging on outreach.
- **Diagnosis.** When revenue dips, the tracker tells you exactly where the pipeline is leaking — not enough outreach? Low response rates? Proposals not closing?

This document gives you the complete framework. You can implement it in a spreadsheet (Google Sheets or Excel), Notion, Airtable, or even a pen-and-paper system. The tool does not matter. Consistency does.

## Pipeline Stages

Every prospect moves through these stages. A prospect can only be in ONE stage at a time. When they take the next action, move them forward. If they go dark, leave them in their current stage until you've completed your follow-up sequence — then move them to Lost.

| Stage | Definition | What Moves Them to the Next Stage |
|-------|------------|-----------------------------------|
| **1. Prospect** | You've identified them as a potential client. You have their name, company, and contact info. You haven't reached out yet. | You send your first outreach email or message. |
| **2. Contacted** | You've sent at least one outreach message (cold email, LinkedIn DM, warm introduction). No response yet. | They reply to your message (positive or neutral). |
| **3. Responded** | They've replied. The conversation is active, but no call is scheduled yet. | A discovery call is booked on the calendar. |
| **4. Call Scheduled** | A discovery or introductory call is on the calendar. | The call takes place. |
| **5. Call Complete** | You've had the discovery call. You're preparing or have sent a proposal. | You send the proposal. |
| **6. Proposal Sent** | The proposal is in their hands. Awaiting their decision. | They respond with questions, negotiate, or accept. |
| **7. Negotiating** | Active discussion about scope, price, timeline, or terms. | They accept and sign the contract. |
| **8. Won** | Contract signed, first payment received. This is now a client. | N/A — move to your project management system. |
| **9. Lost** | They said no, went with someone else, went dark after full follow-up sequence, or you disqualified them. | N/A — log the reason and move on. |

## Weekly Review: The Friday Pipeline Check

Every Friday, spend 20-30 minutes reviewing your pipeline. This is the single most important habit for consistent revenue. Block it on your calendar and treat it as non-negotiable.

### The Friday Review Checklist

**Step 1: Update every active prospect (5-10 minutes)**

- [ ] Go through each prospect in your tracker.
- [ ] Update the Stage if anything has changed this week.
- [ ] Update the Last Activity Date.
- [ ] Update the Next Action with a specific task and date.
- [ ] Remove or archive any prospects that have been in the Lost stage for more than 30 days.

**Step 2: Review your numbers (5 minutes)**

Fill in this week's KPI dashboard (see below).

**Step 3: Identify stalled deals (5 minutes)**

- [ ] Flag any prospect who has been in the same stage for more than 7 business days without activity.
- [ ] For each stalled prospect, decide: follow up now, schedule a follow-up for next week, or move to Lost.
- [ ] If a proposal has been sitting for more than 5 business days without a response, send a follow-up.

**Step 4: Plan next week's outreach (5-10 minutes)**

- [ ] How many new prospects do you need to add to the top of the pipeline? (See "Calculating Your Required Volume" below.)
- [ ] Identify and research [X] new prospects to contact next week.
- [ ] Draft or customize emails for next week's outreach using the Cold Email Swipe File.
- [ ] Schedule any follow-up emails that are due next week.

**Step 5: Celebrate and reflect (2 minutes)**

- [ ] Note any wins from this week (proposals sent, calls booked, deals closed).
- [ ] Note one thing you want to improve next week (better follow-up speed, more personalized outreach, etc.).

## Calculating Your Required Volume

Work backward from your revenue goal to determine how many prospects you need at the top of the pipeline each week.

### The Formula

```
Monthly revenue goal:                    $[YOUR GOAL]
Average project value:                   $[YOUR AVERAGE]
Projects needed per month:               [GOAL / AVERAGE] = [X] projects

Your proposal → won close rate:          [X]%
Proposals needed per month:              [PROJECTS / CLOSE RATE] = [X] proposals

Your call → proposal rate:               [X]%
Calls needed per month:                  [PROPOSALS / CALL-TO-PROPOSAL RATE] = [X] calls

Your outreach → call rate:               [X]%
Outreach emails needed per month:        [CALLS / OUTREACH-TO-CALL RATE] = [X] emails

Weekly outreach target:                  [MONTHLY EMAILS / 4] = [X] emails per week
```

### Example Calculation

```
Monthly revenue goal:                    $10,000
Average project value:                   $3,000
Projects needed per month:               3.3 (round to 4)

Proposal → won close rate:               40%
Proposals needed per month:              4 / 0.40 = 10 proposals

Call → proposal rate:                    70%
Calls needed per month:                  10 / 0.70 = ~15 calls

Outreach → call rate:                    5% (cold email average)
Outreach emails needed per month:        15 / 0.05 = 300 emails

Weekly outreach target:                  300 / 4 = 75 emails per week
```

**Your numbers will be different.** The point is to know your numbers so you can work the math instead of hoping for the best. As your brand grows and referrals increase, you'll need less cold outreach to hit the same numbers.

## Setting This Up: Implementation Guide

### Option 1: Google Sheets (Recommended for simplicity)

**Setup time:** 15 minutes

1. Create a new Google Sheet.
2. Name the first tab "Pipeline" and create these columns:
   - A: Prospect Name
   - B: Company
   - C: Source
   - D: Stage (use a dropdown: Prospect, Contacted, Responded, Call Scheduled, Call Complete, Proposal Sent, Negotiating, Won, Lost)
   - E: First Contact Date
   - F: Last Activity Date
   - G: Next Action
   - H: Estimated Value (format as currency)
   - I: Notes
   - J: Outcome
3. Name the second tab "Weekly KPIs" and recreate the weekly tracking table from above. Add a new row each week.
4. Name the third tab "Monthly Review" and paste the monthly review template.
5. Color-code the Stage column: green for Won, red for Lost, yellow for stages with no activity in 7+ days.
6. Add a filter so you can view only active prospects (hide Won and Lost).
7. Bookmark the sheet and open it every Friday for your weekly review.

**Pro tip:** Use conditional formatting to automatically highlight rows where Last Activity Date is more than 7 days ago. This makes stalled deals visible at a glance.

### Option 2: Notion

**Setup time:** 20-30 minutes

1. Create a new Notion database called "Pipeline."
2. Add these properties:
   - Name (title) — Prospect name
   - Company (text)
   - Source (select: Cold Email, LinkedIn, Referral, Inbound, Event, Other)
   - Stage (select: Prospect, Contacted, Responded, Call Scheduled, Call Complete, Proposal Sent, Negotiating, Won, Lost)
   - First Contact (date)
   - Last Activity (date)
   - Next Action (text)
   - Estimated Value (number, format as USD)
   - Notes (text, long form)
   - Outcome (select: Won, Lost, Deferred, Active)
3. Create a Board View grouped by Stage. This gives you a Kanban-style pipeline where you can drag prospects between stages.
4. Create a Table View filtered to show only active prospects (Stage is not Won or Lost).
5. Create a separate page called "Weekly Reviews" where you paste the weekly KPI template each Friday.
6. Create a separate page called "Monthly Reviews" for your monthly analysis.

**Pro tip:** Use Notion's formula property to calculate "Days in Current Stage" by subtracting the Last Activity date from today. Sort by this column to find stalled deals quickly.

### Option 3: Airtable

**Setup time:** 20-30 minutes

1. Create a new Airtable base called "Client Pipeline."
2. Create a table with the same fields as the Google Sheets setup above.
3. Use Airtable's built-in Kanban view to visualize the pipeline by Stage.
4. Create a Gallery view for a quick overview of each prospect with key details.
5. Use Airtable's automation features to send yourself a reminder when a prospect has been in the same stage for more than 7 days.
6. Create a second table called "Weekly KPIs" to log your numbers each week.

### Option 4: Simple Text File / Paper

If you have fewer than 20 active prospects at any time, a simple system works:

1. Keep a running list in a text file, notes app, or physical notebook.
2. Group prospects by stage.
3. Each Friday, rewrite the list with updated stages and next actions.
4. Track your weekly KPIs at the bottom of the same document.

The tool does not matter. The weekly habit does.

## Common Pipeline Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Not enough prospects entering the pipeline | You're not doing enough outreach, or your targeting is too narrow | Increase weekly outreach volume. Expand your ideal client definition. Try a new outreach channel. |
| Low response rates (below 5%) | Your emails are too generic, your subject lines are weak, or you're targeting the wrong people | Personalize more aggressively. A/B test subject lines. Verify your prospect list matches your ideal client profile. |
| Responses but no calls booked | Your follow-up is too slow, or your reply doesn't include a clear CTA | Reply to every response within 4 hours. Always include a specific time suggestion for a call. Use Calendly or Cal.com to reduce scheduling friction. |
| Calls booked but proposals not sent | You're doing discovery calls that don't lead anywhere, or you're procrastinating on proposals | Improve your qualification questions (see Discovery Call Script). Set a personal rule: proposal sent within 48 hours of every qualified call. |
| Proposals sent but not closing | Your pricing is off, your proposals are generic, or you're not following up | Use the 3-tier proposal framework. Include specific results and ROI projections. Follow up 3 business days after sending. Offer to walk them through the proposal on a call. |
| Pipeline is feast or famine | You stop outreach when you're busy with client work | Never stop outreach. Even during busy periods, send a minimum of [X] emails per week. Batch your outreach on Monday mornings. |
