# Client Onboarding Checklist

## A Complete System for Starting Every Project on the Right Foot

**Part of The Client Machine: The AI-Powered Client Acquisition System for Freelancers**

---

## Why Onboarding Matters

The first two weeks of a client engagement set the tone for the entire relationship. A smooth onboarding process:

- **Builds confidence.** The client knows they made the right choice hiring you.
- **Prevents scope creep.** Clear expectations documented upfront are your best defense.
- **Reduces friction.** Getting access, tools, and communication sorted now means fewer interruptions later.
- **Creates momentum.** A strong start leads to a strong project.

Most freelancers wing it. You won't. Use this checklist for every new client.

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Kickoff Preparation

Complete these items after the contract is signed and the first payment is received, but before the kickoff call.

### Checklist

- [ ] **Contract fully signed and filed.** Save the signed contract in your project folder. Confirm both parties have a copy.
- [ ] **First payment received and confirmed.** Verify the payment has cleared before beginning any work. Send a receipt or confirmation if your invoicing tool doesn't do it automatically.
- [ ] **Project folder created.** Set up your folder structure for the project:
  ```
  /[CLIENT NAME] - [PROJECT NAME]/
    /01-Contract & Invoice/
    /02-Discovery & Research/
    /03-Deliverables/
    /04-Client Assets (logos, brand guides, access credentials)/
    /05-Communication & Notes/
    /06-Final Delivery/
  ```
- [ ] **Review discovery call notes.** Re-read your discovery call notes and proposal to refresh yourself on the scope, key pain points, success metrics, and any specific concerns the client raised.
- [ ] **Prepare the onboarding questionnaire.** Customize the questionnaire (see below) based on the project type. Remove irrelevant questions and add project-specific ones.
- [ ] **Send the welcome email.** (See Welcome Email Template below.) This should go out within 24 hours of contract signing.
- [ ] **Schedule the kickoff call.** Include a calendar invite with a clear agenda and any pre-call instructions (such as filling out the onboarding questionnaire beforehand).

Send this email within 24 hours of the contract being signed. It sets the professional tone and gives the client clear instructions on what happens next.

## Phase 3: Kickoff Meeting Agenda

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Attendees:** You + the client's primary point of contact (and any other stakeholders)

### Agenda

**1. Introductions & rapport (5 minutes)**
- If there are new stakeholders on the call who weren't in the discovery phase, do brief introductions.
- Quick personal check-in to set a collaborative tone.

**2. Project overview and goals (10 minutes)**
- Restate the project scope, key deliverables, and success metrics as you understand them.
- Ask: "Has anything changed since our last conversation? Any new priorities or constraints?"
- Confirm the primary goal: "If this project achieves one thing, what's the most important outcome?"

**3. Walk through the timeline and milestones (10 minutes)**
- Review the timeline from the proposal phase by phase.
- Confirm key dates: when you'll deliver each milestone, when the client needs to provide feedback.
- Agree on the feedback turnaround time: "I'll ask for feedback at specific checkpoints. Can we agree that feedback will come back within [X BUSINESS DAYS]? Delays in feedback will push subsequent milestones."

**4. Communication and working process (10 minutes)**
- Confirm the communication channel (email, Slack, project board, etc.).
- Confirm the meeting cadence: weekly check-ins, async updates, or both.
- Discuss how you handle revisions: "The scope includes [X] rounds of revisions per phase. Here's how the revision process works: [DESCRIBE YOUR PROCESS — e.g., 'I'll deliver the work, you'll compile all feedback into one document, and I'll implement the changes in one round.']"
- Clarify the escalation path: "If something urgent comes up that can't wait for our weekly check-in, what's the best way to reach you? And who should I contact if you're unavailable?"

**5. Review the onboarding questionnaire (5-10 minutes)**
- Go through their questionnaire responses and clarify anything that's ambiguous.
- Ask follow-up questions on any answers that need more detail.

**6. Access and tools setup (5-10 minutes)**
- Confirm you have all necessary access (see Access & Tools Checklist below).
- Walk through any access that still needs to be set up.
- Test access live on the call if possible.

**7. Questions and wrap-up (5 minutes)**
- "Do you have any questions or concerns before we start?"
- "Is there anything I should know that we haven't covered?"
- Confirm the first deliverable date and what they can expect from you in the first week.

## Phase 5: Access & Tools Setup Checklist

Customize this checklist based on your service type. Send it as part of the onboarding questionnaire or review it during the kickoff call.

### General (all project types)
- [ ] Primary communication channel set up (Slack channel created, email threads started, etc.)
- [ ] Project management board created and client invited ([TOOL — e.g., Trello, Asana, Notion, Linear])
- [ ] Shared file storage set up (Google Drive folder, Dropbox, etc.)
- [ ] Calendar invites sent for all scheduled calls

### For web design / development projects
- [ ] Access to current website CMS (WordPress, Webflow, Squarespace, etc.)
- [ ] Hosting account access or staging environment credentials
- [ ] Domain registrar access (if DNS changes are needed)
- [ ] Brand assets: logo files (SVG, PNG), brand colors (hex codes), fonts, brand guidelines document
- [ ] Google Analytics / Plausible / other analytics tool access (at minimum, Viewer access)
- [ ] Google Search Console access
- [ ] Any existing design files (Figma, Sketch, Adobe XD)

### For marketing / content projects
- [ ] Access to CMS / blog publishing platform
- [ ] Social media account credentials or manager access
- [ ] Email marketing platform access (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, etc.)
- [ ] SEO tools access (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Search Console)
- [ ] Analytics access (Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude)
- [ ] Editorial calendar or content planning documents
- [ ] Brand voice guide or past content examples

### For conversion optimization / growth projects
- [ ] Analytics access (Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, etc.)
- [ ] A/B testing tool access (Optimizely, VWO, Google Optimize successor, etc.)
- [ ] Heatmap tool access (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity, FullStory)
- [ ] CRM or sales tool access (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive)
- [ ] Access to current landing pages and funnel pages
- [ ] Historical performance data (conversion rates, traffic sources, revenue data)

## Phase 6: First Milestone Delivery Plan

The first deliverable sets the tone. Deliver it on time (or early) and exceed expectations. Here's how to structure your approach to the first milestone.

### Week 1 Plan

**Days 1-2: Deep dive**
- [ ] Review all questionnaire responses, access credentials, and existing materials.
- [ ] Conduct your own research: audit the current state, analyze competitors, review data.
- [ ] Identify any gaps in information and send the client a concise list of follow-up questions.

**Days 3-4: First draft work**
- [ ] Begin work on the first deliverable.
- [ ] If you encounter any blockers (missing access, unclear direction), flag them immediately — do not wait for the weekly check-in.
- [ ] Send a mid-week progress ping: "Hi [NAME], quick update — I'm on track for [FIRST MILESTONE]. I've been digging into [WHAT YOU'VE BEEN WORKING ON] and I'm seeing some interesting [FINDINGS / OPPORTUNITIES]. More to come on [DELIVERY DAY]."

**Day 5: Quality check and delivery**
- [ ] Review your work against the scope document. Does it match what was agreed?
- [ ] Proofread everything. Check for typos, broken links, formatting issues.
- [ ] Prepare a brief summary to accompany the deliverable: what you did, what you found, and what it means.
- [ ] Deliver the first milestone with context (not just a file drop — explain what they're looking at and what you recommend).

### First Delivery Email Template

> **Subject:** [DELIVERABLE NAME] — ready for review
>
> Hi [CLIENT NAME],
>
> Attached / linked is the [FIRST DELIVERABLE NAME — e.g., "conversion audit report," "initial wireframes," "content strategy framework"].
>
> **Here's a quick summary of what you'll find:**
> - [KEY FINDING / ITEM 1 — e.g., "3 high-impact opportunities in your signup funnel, prioritized by expected ROI"]
> - [KEY FINDING / ITEM 2 — e.g., "Competitive analysis showing where you're ahead and where you're behind"]
> - [KEY FINDING / ITEM 3 — e.g., "Recommended approach for Phase 2, with two options to consider"]
>
> **What I need from you:**
> Please review by [DATE — 2-3 business days]. When you're ready, you can share your feedback via [METHOD — e.g., "comments in the Google Doc," "a reply to this email," "our Slack channel"].
>
> I'd suggest focusing on:
> 1. Does the overall direction feel right?
> 2. Is anything missing that you expected to see?
> 3. Any specific areas where you want me to go deeper?
>
> We can discuss during our next check-in on [DAY], or feel free to reach out anytime if you'd rather chat sooner.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

## Phase 7: 30-Day Check-In Template

At the 30-day mark (or the project midpoint for shorter engagements), conduct a structured check-in to make sure the project is on track and the client is satisfied. This catches small problems before they become big ones.

### 30-Day Check-In Email

> **Subject:** 30-day project check-in
>
> Hi [CLIENT NAME],
>
> We're [30 DAYS / HALFWAY / X WEEKS] into our engagement, and I wanted to take a moment to check in on how things are going.
>
> **What we've accomplished so far:**
> - [COMPLETED ITEM 1 — e.g., "Completed the full conversion audit and identified 12 optimization opportunities"]
> - [COMPLETED ITEM 2 — e.g., "Delivered wireframes and copy for the redesigned signup flow (approved after one round of revisions)"]
> - [COMPLETED ITEM 3 — e.g., "Began implementation in Webflow — staging site is live for review"]
>
> **What's coming next:**
> - [UPCOMING ITEM 1 — e.g., "Final implementation and QA testing (Week 5)"]
> - [UPCOMING ITEM 2 — e.g., "A/B test launch and monitoring (Week 6)"]
>
> **A few questions for you:**
> 1. Is the project meeting your expectations so far? Anything I could be doing better?
> 2. Have any new priorities or changes come up on your end that I should know about?
> 3. Are you happy with our communication cadence, or would you like more/less frequent updates?
>
> I'd love to hear your honest feedback — positive or constructive. It helps me deliver the best possible result for [THEIR COMPANY NAME].
>
> Happy to discuss on our next call, or feel free to reply here.
>
> [YOUR NAME]

### What to Do With the Feedback

| Feedback Type | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| **Positive feedback** | Thank them, stay the course, make a note for a testimonial request later. |
| **Minor adjustments** | Implement immediately. Reply confirming the change: "Great point — I've adjusted [THING]. Here's the updated approach." |
| **Significant concerns** | Schedule a call within 24 hours. Do not try to resolve major issues over email. Listen, acknowledge, and propose a specific plan to address the concern. |
| **Scope change requests** | Evaluate whether the request fits within the current scope. If not, reply: "Great idea. That's outside the current scope, but I can add it as a separate line item. Let me put together a quick estimate for you." |
| **No response** | Follow up in 2-3 days: "Hi [NAME], just wanted to make sure my check-in didn't get buried. I'd love your feedback so I can make sure the rest of the project is exactly what you need." |

## Onboarding Questionnaire Template

Send this with the welcome email. Customize based on your service type.

### General Questions (all project types)

1. What is the single most important outcome you want from this project?
2. Who is your target audience or ideal customer? Describe them in as much detail as possible.
3. Who are your top 3 competitors? What do they do well? What do they do poorly?
4. Are there any examples (from your industry or outside it) of what you want this to look or feel like?
5. Are there any internal deadlines, launch dates, or events we should be aware of?
6. Who will be the primary point of contact for feedback and approvals? Is there anyone else who needs to review deliverables?
7. Is there anything you've tried before that didn't work? What should I avoid?
8. What does your team need to stay informed about this project? Should anyone be CC'd on updates?

### Access Checklist (include the relevant items from Phase 5 above)

9. Please provide access to the following tools and platforms: [LIST BASED ON PROJECT TYPE]
10. Please share any existing brand assets: [LOGO FILES, BRAND GUIDE, FONTS, COLOR CODES]
