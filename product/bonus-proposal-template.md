## How to Use This Template

This proposal template is designed to be customized for every prospective client. It follows a proven structure that walks the client from "I understand your problem" to "Here's how I'll solve it" to "Here's what it costs" to "Here's what to do next."

**Before you write:**
1. Complete a discovery call (use the Discovery Call Script bonus).
2. Review your notes and identify the client's top 2-3 pain points.
3. Choose the pricing tier structure that fits the project.
4. Draft the proposal within 48 hours of the discovery call — speed signals professionalism.

**Formatting tips:**
- Send as a PDF or a clean Google Doc (not a Word file).
- Use their brand colors for the cover page if possible.
- Keep the entire proposal under 5 pages. Shorter proposals close faster.
- Use bullet points and bold text liberally — decision-makers skim, they don't read.

## 1. Executive Summary

[Write 3-5 sentences that summarize the entire proposal. This section should answer three questions: What is the problem? What are you going to do about it? What result can they expect? Many decision-makers will only read this section, so make it count.]

[CLIENT COMPANY NAME] is looking to [THEIR GOAL — e.g., "increase conversion rates on their self-serve signup flow," "build a content engine that drives qualified inbound leads," "redesign the mobile experience to improve retention"]. Currently, [THEIR CORE PROBLEM — e.g., "the signup-to-activation rate sits at 12%, well below the industry benchmark of 25-30%," "organic traffic has plateaued at 8,000 visits/month with no clear content strategy," "the mobile bounce rate is 68%, suggesting the current experience is driving users away"].

I propose a [DURATION — e.g., "6-week"] engagement focused on [HIGH-LEVEL DESCRIPTION OF YOUR APPROACH — e.g., "auditing the current funnel, redesigning the key conversion points, and implementing A/B tests to validate improvements"]. Based on my experience with similar projects, [CLIENT COMPANY NAME] can expect [EXPECTED OUTCOME — e.g., "a 15-30% improvement in signup-to-activation conversion within 90 days of implementation"].

## 2. Problem Statement

[This section demonstrates that you listened during the discovery call and understand their world. Use their own words wherever possible. Do NOT describe your solution here — only describe the problem.]

### What I heard from you:

Based on our conversation on [DATE OF DISCOVERY CALL], here is my understanding of the challenges [CLIENT COMPANY NAME] is facing:

**Primary challenge:**
[DESCRIBE THE MAIN PROBLEM IN 2-3 SENTENCES — e.g., "Your team is generating strong top-of-funnel traffic through paid campaigns, but the conversion rate from visitor to trial signup is significantly below benchmarks. This means you're paying to acquire visitors who leave without taking action."]

**Contributing factors:**
- [FACTOR 1 — e.g., "The landing page requires too many steps before signup (currently 3 pages before the user reaches the registration form)."]
- [FACTOR 2 — e.g., "There is no social proof or case study content visible during the signup flow."]
- [FACTOR 3 — e.g., "The value proposition on the landing page focuses on features rather than outcomes."]
- [FACTOR 4 — optional, add if relevant]

**Business impact:**
[QUANTIFY THE COST OF THE PROBLEM — e.g., "Based on the numbers you shared, the current conversion rate gap represents approximately $[X] in lost monthly revenue. Over the next 12 months, this compounds to $[X] in unrealized growth, not counting the wasted ad spend driving traffic that doesn't convert."]

[Guidance: If you can't quantify in dollars, quantify in time, missed opportunities, or competitive disadvantage. The point is to make the cost of inaction tangible.]

## 3. Proposed Solution

[This is where you describe WHAT you will do, HOW you will do it, and WHAT they will receive. Be specific enough that the client can visualize the work, but not so detailed that you're giving away your entire methodology for free.]

### Approach

I will [HIGH-LEVEL APPROACH — e.g., "conduct a full audit of the current conversion funnel, identify the highest-impact improvement opportunities, redesign the key pages, write new conversion-focused copy, and implement A/B testing to validate the changes"].

### Scope of Work

**Phase 1: [PHASE NAME — e.g., "Research & Audit"] ([DURATION — e.g., "Week 1-2"])**
- [DELIVERABLE 1 — e.g., "Comprehensive audit of the current signup funnel with heatmap and analytics analysis"]
- [DELIVERABLE 2 — e.g., "Competitive analysis of 5 similar products' conversion flows"]
- [DELIVERABLE 3 — e.g., "Prioritized list of improvement opportunities ranked by expected impact and effort"]
- **Deliverable:** [WHAT THEY RECEIVE — e.g., "Audit report with recommendations (PDF/Google Doc)"]

**Phase 2: [PHASE NAME — e.g., "Strategy & Design"] ([DURATION — e.g., "Week 3-4"])**
- [DELIVERABLE 1 — e.g., "Wireframes for the redesigned signup flow (2 variant options)"]
- [DELIVERABLE 2 — e.g., "Conversion-focused copywriting for all landing page elements"]
- [DELIVERABLE 3 — e.g., "New social proof and trust element integration plan"]
- **Deliverable:** [WHAT THEY RECEIVE — e.g., "Design mockups and copy document for review and approval"]

**Phase 3: [PHASE NAME — e.g., "Implementation & Testing"] ([DURATION — e.g., "Week 5-6"])**
- [DELIVERABLE 1 — e.g., "Implementation of approved designs in [PLATFORM — e.g., Webflow, WordPress, custom code]"]
- [DELIVERABLE 2 — e.g., "A/B test setup comparing current vs. new signup flow"]
- [DELIVERABLE 3 — e.g., "Analytics and tracking configuration to measure results"]
- **Deliverable:** [WHAT THEY RECEIVE — e.g., "Live implementation with A/B testing running"]

### What's Included
- [ITEM — e.g., "All copy, design, and implementation work"]
- [ITEM — e.g., "Up to [X] rounds of revisions per phase"]
- [ITEM — e.g., "Weekly progress updates via email + one 30-minute check-in call per week"]
- [ITEM — e.g., "30-day post-launch monitoring and one round of optimization adjustments"]

### What's Not Included
[Be explicit about boundaries to prevent scope creep.]
- [ITEM — e.g., "Paid advertising management or ad creative"]
- [ITEM — e.g., "Backend development or database changes"]
- [ITEM — e.g., "Ongoing maintenance after the 30-day post-launch period"]
- [ITEM — e.g., "Additional pages or flows beyond the scope defined above"]

### Payment Terms

[CHOOSE ONE OR CUSTOMIZE:]

## Appendix: AI Prompt for Generating a First Draft

Use this prompt after your discovery call to generate a first draft of your proposal. Paste your discovery call notes into the prompt, then edit the output to match your voice and add specific details.

```
I need to write a client proposal based on my discovery call notes. Please generate a complete first draft using the structure below.

**My information:**
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My service/specialty: [WHAT YOU DO]
- My relevant experience: [2-3 PAST PROJECTS OR RESULTS]
- My website/portfolio: [URL]

**Client information:**
- Client name: [CLIENT CONTACT NAME]
- Client company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Client's role: [TITLE]
- Industry: [THEIR INDUSTRY]

**Discovery call notes:**
[PASTE YOUR RAW NOTES FROM THE DISCOVERY CALL HERE — include everything: their problems, goals, current situation, budget range, timeline, decision-making process, competitors, previous attempts to solve the problem, and any specific requirements or constraints they mentioned]

**Pricing:**
- Tier 1 (basic/strategy only): $[AMOUNT]
- Tier 2 (recommended/full execution): $[AMOUNT]
- Tier 3 (premium/ongoing partnership): $[AMOUNT]

**Project duration:** [ESTIMATED TIMELINE]

**Instructions:**
Using the information above, generate a complete proposal with these sections:
1. Executive Summary (3-5 sentences)
2. Problem Statement (use the client's own words where possible, quantify the business impact)
3. Proposed Solution (break into phases with specific deliverables)
4. Timeline & Milestones (weekly breakdown with client action items)
5. Investment (3-tier options with clear descriptions of what's included in each)
6. About Me (focus on relevant experience)
7. Terms & Next Steps

Write in a professional but conversational tone. Be specific, not generic. Use the client's company name and specific details from the discovery call throughout. Keep the entire proposal under 2,000 words.
```

**After the AI generates the draft:**
1. Read every line and replace generic language with specific details.
2. Make sure the Problem Statement uses the client's actual words from your call notes.
3. Verify that every deliverable in the Scope of Work is something you can actually deliver.
4. Double-check your pricing and make sure the tier descriptions are accurate.
5. Add your testimonials and portfolio links manually.
6. Read it one final time from the client's perspective — does it make them feel understood?
